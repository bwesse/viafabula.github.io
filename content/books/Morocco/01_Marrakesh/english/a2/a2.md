My Journey to Marrakesh

Last March, I finally fulfilled my dream of visiting Marrakesh. I had been planning this trip for over a year, and the excitement was overwhelming as my plane descended over the red city.

The moment I stepped out of the airport, I felt the warm Moroccan sun on my face. A taxi driver named Hassan greeted me with a friendly smile and drove me to my riad in the medina. The narrow streets were too small for cars, so I had to walk the last part with my luggage.

My riad was like a hidden palace. From the outside, it looked like any other building, but inside, there was a beautiful courtyard with a fountain, orange trees, and colorful tiles everywhere. The owner, Fatima, welcomed me with mint tea and pastries.

The next morning, I woke up early to explore. The medina was already buzzing with life. Shopkeepers were opening their stores, and the smell of fresh bread filled the air. I wandered through the souks, where craftsmen were making leather goods, lamps, and carpets by hand.

At midday, I reached Jemaa el-Fnaa square. What an incredible place! There were snake charmers, musicians, storytellers, and acrobats performing everywhere. I watched a group of Gnawa musicians playing their distinctive music while people gathered around them.

For lunch, I tried my first tagine at a small restaurant recommended by Fatima. The lamb was so tender it fell off the bone, and the combination of spices was unlike anything I had tasted before. I also learned to eat with my right hand, using bread to scoop up the food, following local customs.
