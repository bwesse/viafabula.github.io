## Chapter: Morocco, Where the World Keeps Arriving

Morocco is a country. It is in North Africa. It has the ocean and the sea. Europe is near. Morocco has mountains too.

People lived in Morocco long ago. The Amazigh people lived there. They had their own language and life. Later, other people came too.

Morocco had trade on the coast. Carthage had contact with North Africa. Later, Rome came to some places. Rome built roads and towns.

We can see Roman ruins today. Volubilis is a place with old Roman buildings. It shows people lived there long ago.

Later, Islam came to North Africa. Many people became Muslim. There were also fights and revolts.

Then Morocco had early rulers. The Idrisid family ruled in the north. This helped build early Morocco.

Fez became an important city. People studied there. It became a famous place.

Later, the Almoravids became strong. They built Marrakesh. Marrakesh became important.

After them, the Almohads became strong too. They ruled Morocco and other lands.

Morocco had many changes. Sometimes there were conflicts. But Morocco stayed and continued.

Morocco grew in culture. People built buildings. Crafts and trade were important.

Later, Europe became stronger by sea. European powers wanted ports. The Saʿdi rulers became strong in Morocco.

Ahmad al-Mansur was a famous ruler. Morocco was strong in his time.

After that, Morocco had problems again. Then the ʿAlawite family ruled. They still rule today.

Later, France and Spain controlled parts of Morocco. Morocco had less freedom.

Moroccans wanted independence. Many people worked against colonial rule.

In 1956, Morocco became independent. Muhammad V was important.

After that, Hassan II ruled for many years. Morocco changed a lot in this time.

Western Sahara became an important issue. In 1975, there was the Green March. The problem continued.

Western Sahara is still important today. It affects Morocco’s politics.

In 1999, Mohammed VI became king. Morocco continued to modernise.

Morocco has different regions. Coast, mountains, and desert are different. Amazigh and Arabic-Islamic culture are important.

Tangier is a city in the north. It has strong links to Europe. The coast connects Morocco to the world.

Morocco had many rulers and many changes. Other powers came. But Morocco stayed itself.

Morocco took influence from others. But it kept its identity. The world came, and Morocco stayed Morocco.
