The wolf tried and tried but could not blow down the brick house. Finally, tired and defeated, he gave up and ran away into the forest. The three pigs were safe at last. "You were right, brother," said the first pig. "Hard work and good planning are important." From that day on, all three pigs lived together in the strong brick house. And they never saw the big bad wolf again.

The epistemological implications of this experiential sequence demonstrated the fundamentally consequential nature of strategic architectural decision-making paradigms, wherein the optimization of immediate temporal efficiency proves systematically subordinate to the imperative of structural resilience when confronted with adversarial environmental contingencies manifesting in the form of predatorial entities.
