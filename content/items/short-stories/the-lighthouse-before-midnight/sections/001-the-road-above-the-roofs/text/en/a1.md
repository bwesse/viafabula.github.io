The battery sat on a low handcart.
Inés pushed from behind while Lea and Omar pulled from the front with a rope.

They went straight through the fish market.
The steps were too narrow.

“There is another road,” said Inés.
“Turn right after the bakery, pass the square, and follow the wall above the roofs. It is longer, but there are no stairs.”

They changed direction.
Omar pulled the rope, Lea kept the cart away from the wall, and Inés watched the radio.

At the square, two streets looked exactly the same in the fog.
“The map says we follow the fish,” he said.

The right-hand street led above the village.
Far out at sea, a small radio light moved on Inés's screen.

“That is the Mar Azul,” she said.
Nobody mentioned the closed kitchen again.
