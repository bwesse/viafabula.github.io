The final path crossed an open hill. Halfway up, a fallen pine tree blocked the road. The cart could not go over it, and the ground on both sides was soft with rain. From that moment, the story moved in a new direction.

Lea found a narrow space between the tree and a rock. Omar tied their rope around the battery. Inés guided the wheels while they pulled the cart sideways, one careful meter at a time.

The cart became stuck in the mud. For a moment, nobody moved. Then Lea placed three flat stones under the wheels. Omar pushed, Inés pulled, and the cart rolled onto the path again.

They reached the lighthouse at 11:32. Inside, a spiral staircase climbed around the stone wall. Together they carried the battery up, resting after every twelve steps.

At the top, Inés opened the metal box below the lamp. Her face changed. “The connector cable is not here,” she said. “It must still be in the tool shed beside the lower gate.”

Omar looked down the long staircase. Lea took the flashlight. “Tell us exactly where it is,” she said. Inés stayed to prepare the lamp while Lea and Omar ran back outside.

They followed the lighthouse wall to the left, passed a water tank, and found the lower gate. The shed was locked, but the key hung under a red cup, exactly where Inés had said. Inside, the cable lay on a shelf marked EMERGENCY.
