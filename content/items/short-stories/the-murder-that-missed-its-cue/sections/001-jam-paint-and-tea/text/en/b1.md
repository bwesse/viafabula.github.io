Lina asked everyone to stay in the main hall. “If Felix is hurt, we must find him quickly,” she said. “If he is acting, he can explain why he took the money.” The situation was beginning to change.

First, she touched the red stain with a paper napkin and smelled it. It was sweet. “Raspberry jam,” said Pablo, the baker. “It is from my cake.”

Pablo explained that Nora had borrowed a jar before the party. “She said the theatre blood was too dark,” he added. Nora nodded. “The jam was for the planned scene. That does not explain the missing box.”

Next, Lina found a white glove beside the curtain. It belonged to Felix, but there was fresh blue paint on its fingers. Amira, the costume designer, looked worried.

“I painted the handle of the trapdoor blue at six o'clock,” Amira said. “It should have been dry by tomorrow. Nobody was supposed to touch it tonight.” The trapdoor was hidden under a round carpet near the silver box.

The cup of peppermint tea was still warm. Lina asked when Felix had made it. “Just before he went onto the stage,” said Pablo. That meant Felix had been behind the curtain only a few minutes earlier.

So the blood was not blood, the glove had touched the trapdoor, and Felix had not left long ago. Lina now believed he was alive. But why were Nora's hands shaking?
