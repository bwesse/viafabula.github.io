The wet footprints seemed to lead from the stage to the garden door. Outside, however, the stones were dry under the roof. The rain could not have made those marks. In retrospect, the consequences of that moment were already taking shape.

Amira showed Lina a pair of large costume boots. One boot had a square nail in its heel. Every wet print on the floor had the same little square. Someone had used the boots and a glass of water to make a false trail.

Beside the final footprint, Lina found a green pencil. Nora always used green pencils to write notes during rehearsals. Nora said, “Many people borrow them.” It was true, but she looked toward the balcony while she spoke.

The clock on the stage showed 8:10, the supposed time of the murder. The clock in the hall showed 8:38. Lina checked the stage clock and discovered that it had no battery. It was only another theatre object.

Under the clock was a small note: AFTER THE THIRD BELL, LOOK UP. The words were written in green. A little silver dust covered one corner of the paper.

Lina saw the same silver dust on Nora's sleeve. She also noticed a small key on a chain around Nora's neck. “The key to the trapdoor?” Lina asked. Nora covered it with her hand.

Before Lina could say more, the theatre bell began to move. It rang once. Everyone became quiet. It rang a second time. Lina walked to the middle of the stage and looked up.
