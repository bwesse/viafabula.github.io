The Lantern Theatre was holding a costume party to collect money for a new roof.
Guests wore masks, drank fruit punch, and put donations into a silver box beside the stage.

Lina was helping at the door.
The theatre director, Nora, gave every guest a card: AT THE SECOND BELL, A CRIME WILL BEGIN.

At eight o'clock, the first bell rang.
He complained about the cold, drank peppermint tea, and told everyone that he had many enemies.

At half past eight, the second bell rang.
Several guests screamed, and then remembered that this was a game.

The lights went out for ten seconds.
The silver donation box had disappeared too.

Nora came onto the stage.
She tried to smile, but her voice sounded serious.

The guests stopped laughing.
The planned crime had suddenly become a real mystery.
