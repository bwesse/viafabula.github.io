The Lantern Theatre was holding a costume party to collect money for a new roof. Guests wore masks, drank fruit punch, and put donations into a silver box beside the stage. In retrospect, the consequences of that moment were already taking shape.

Lina was helping at the door. She loved mystery stories, although she had never solved a real mystery. The theatre director, Nora, gave every guest a card: AT THE SECOND BELL, A CRIME WILL BEGIN.

At eight o'clock, the first bell rang. Felix, the theatre's most dramatic actor, walked onto the stage dressed as Professor Moon. He complained about the cold, drank peppermint tea, and told everyone that he had many enemies.

At half past eight, the second bell rang. Felix put one hand on his heart and fell beside the silver box. A red stain spread across his white shirt. Several guests screamed, and then remembered that this was a game.

The lights went out for ten seconds. Someone moved behind the curtain. When the lights returned, Felix was gone. The silver donation box had disappeared too.

Nora came onto the stage. “Felix should still be here,” she said. “And the box was not part of the game.” She tried to smile, but her voice sounded serious.

The guests stopped laughing. Lina looked at the red stain on the floor, a wet footprint near the garden door, and a cup of tea behind the curtain. The planned crime had suddenly become a real mystery.
