On the morning of the dinner, Sofia carried the finished table into the courtyard.
Sunlight touched the wood, and for one happy moment it looked exactly as she had imagined.

Then Malik placed a bowl on it.
He pressed one corner, and the table answered with a small knock against the ground.

“One leg is a little short,” he said.
“No, thank you. I know how to fix my own work.”

She carried the table back inside and measured every leg.
Now another leg seemed too long.

She measured again, cut again, and checked the floor.
The workshop floor was not level, but Sofia blamed the table and then herself.

At five o'clock, music started in the courtyard.
She looked at the unfinished table and thought, “Everyone will see the mistake before they see anything else.”

Someone knocked.
“May we carry it together this time?”
