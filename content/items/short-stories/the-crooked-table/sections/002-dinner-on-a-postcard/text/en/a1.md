Sofia wanted to say no.
The four of them carried the table into the courtyard while the paper lanterns moved above their heads.

The table knocked against the ground again.
The table became still.

“That is not a proper repair,” Sofia said.
“It is a proper dinner.”

The guests sat down.
Nobody seemed worried.

During the meal, a child dropped tomato sauce on the new wood.
Sofia opened her mouth, but then she heard the child telling a joke.

The joke was not very good.
Malik was sharing soup, Eva was cutting bread, and the children were explaining how each lantern had received its name.

Sofia sat in the last empty chair.
She ate cold soup because she had arrived late, but it tasted better than the coffee she had drunk alone all week.
