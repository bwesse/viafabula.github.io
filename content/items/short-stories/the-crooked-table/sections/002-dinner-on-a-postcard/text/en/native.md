Sofia wanted to say no. Instead, she stepped aside. The four of them carried the table into the courtyard while the paper lanterns moved above their heads. That was when things started to get interesting.

The table knocked against the ground again. Before Sofia could apologize, Malik folded an old postcard and pushed it under the short leg. The table became still.

“That is not a proper repair,” Sofia said. Malik put the soup on the table. “No,” he agreed. “It's a proper dinner.”

The guests sat down. Their chairs didn't match. One plate had a blue edge, another had yellow flowers, and a third had a small crack. Nobody seemed worried.

During the meal, a child dropped tomato sauce on the new wood. Eva put down a hot bread dish and left a pale round mark. Sofia opened her mouth, but then she heard the child telling a joke.

The joke wasn't very good. Everyone laughed anyway. Sofia looked around the table. Malik was sharing soup, Eva was cutting bread, and the children were explaining how each lantern had received its name.

Sofia sat in the last empty chair. She ate cold soup because she had arrived late, but it tasted better than the coffee she had drunk alone all week.
