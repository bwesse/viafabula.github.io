Sofia repaired old furniture in a small workshop behind her apartment.
Every morning, she opened the green door at seven, made coffee, and placed her tools in a straight line.

She liked work that was exact.
If a line was almost straight, Sofia saw only the part that was not.

In June, the neighbors planned a dinner in the courtyard.
Malik would cook soup, Eva would bring bread, and the children would make paper lanterns.

“We need a long table,” Malik said.
“I will build one.”

The dinner was in two weeks.
Perhaps the neighbors would finally understand why good work needed time.

After that, Sofia stayed in the workshop every evening.
When the children showed her their lanterns, she said, “Later. There is too much dust here.”

The table grew longer and smoother.
Sofia did not notice either change.
