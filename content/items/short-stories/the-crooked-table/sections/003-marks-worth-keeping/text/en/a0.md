The next morning, Sofia returned to the courtyard with sandpaper and a small piece of wood.

The table was covered with signs from the evening: a red drop, the pale circle, two small scratches, and a line from Malik's heavy soup pot.

On the ground, the folded postcard was still under the leg.

Malik came through the courtyard carrying two cups of tea.

“Yes,” she said.

Sofia did not remove all the marks.

That afternoon, she and Malik drank tea at the table.
