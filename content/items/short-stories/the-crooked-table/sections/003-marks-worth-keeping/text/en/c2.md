The next morning, Sofia returned to the courtyard with sandpaper and a small piece of wood. She planned to remove the stains and make a strong support for the short leg. In retrospect, the consequences of that moment were already taking shape.

The table was covered with signs from the evening: a red drop, the pale circle, two small scratches, and a line from Malik's heavy soup pot. Sofia touched each mark.

On the ground, the folded postcard was still under the leg. It showed a beach and the words WISH YOU WERE HERE. Sofia laughed quietly. She placed it in her pocket instead of throwing it away.

Malik came through the courtyard carrying two cups of tea. “Do you want help?” he asked. Sofia almost gave her usual answer.

“Yes,” she said. “Please hold this side.” Together they added a small wooden support. It was simple, strong, and finished in ten minutes.

Sofia did not remove all the marks. She cleaned the table, but left the pale circle. Under it, she wrote the date of the dinner in small letters.

That afternoon, she and Malik drank tea at the table. When the children came with a new lantern, Sofia put down her tools. “Show me now,” she said.
