The conductor took Nina and Pepper to the service compartment. At the next station, an announcement filled the train: “Will the passenger with the stolen blue bag please come to platform three?” That was when things started to get interesting.

Every person near Nina turned to look. “It wasn't stolen,” Nina said. “It was taken by accident.” Pepper sat inside the bag and cleaned a paw. She didn't look innocent.

On platform three, the man with the red hat was waiting with Nina's real bag. Beside him stood a police officer. Nina raised both hands. “I can explain the cat, the sausage, and the bag,” she began. “The shoe is more difficult.”

The man suddenly laughed. “I am Mr. Costa. I told the station worker that my blue bag was missing. I didn't say stolen.” The station worker looked embarrassed. “I wanted the announcement to sound clear,” she said.

Mr. Costa opened Nina's bag. Paper flowers rose out like a colorful cloud. The police officer took one off his hat. Then the golden teapot fell onto the platform with a loud clang.

Nina gave Pepper back and checked the cat carefully. “She is fine,” said Mr. Costa. “She has simply had a more interesting breakfast than usual.” He gave the waiter money for the sausage.

Nina looked at the departure board. Their train had left. The next one was in forty minutes. “Leo will worry,” she said. Mr. Costa looked at the paper flowers and asked, “Leo from the new café?”
