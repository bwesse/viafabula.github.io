Nina opened the bag a few centimeters. Two green eyes looked at her. A small black cat pushed its head through the opening. Around its neck was a label: PEPPER — PLEASE CALL MR. COSTA. Beneath the ordinary surface, the situation was already beginning to shift.

“You are not a paper flower,” Nina whispered. Pepper answered by climbing onto her knees.

Nina wanted to call the number on the label, but her phone went dark. She asked the passenger opposite her, “Excuse me, may I use your phone? I have taken the wrong bag. Also, the bag has a cat.” The passenger moved one seat away.

Before Nina could explain more, a breakfast cart came through the carriage. Pepper smelled a plate of sausages. The cat jumped from Nina's knees, landed on the cart, and ran away with one sausage.

“Stop that cat!” shouted the waiter. “Whose cat is it?” asked the conductor. Three children cheered. Nina ran after Pepper and said, “It is not my cat, but it is my mistake!”

Pepper went under a seat, over a suitcase, and through the open door of the quiet carriage. People looked up from their books. The cat dropped the sausage into the shoe of a sleeping man.

In the end, Nina caught Pepper with help from a little girl and half a cheese sandwich. The conductor crossed her arms. Nina apologized to the waiter, the sleeping man, the quiet carriage, and, finally, the cat.
