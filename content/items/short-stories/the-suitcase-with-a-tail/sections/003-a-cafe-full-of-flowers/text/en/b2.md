Mr. Costa was the musician for the opening. He had planned to take the same train with Pepper, but the cat had refused to stay home. “We are all late now,” he said. “That is almost the same as being on time together.” Already, the situation was becoming more complicated than it first appeared.

They took the next train. Nina held her decorations, Mr. Costa held Pepper, and the golden teapot sat between them. Pepper watched it as if it might run away.

When they reached Leo's café, the guests were already waiting outside. Leo saw the red hat, the blue bags, and the police officer's paper flower, which had somehow traveled with them. “What happened?” he asked.

Nina took a deep breath. “I took the wrong bag. The wrong bag took a sausage. The station called me a thief. Then your musician paid for breakfast.”

Leo was silent for a second. Then he opened the café door and said, “Good. We needed a story for the first customers.” Everyone helped hang the lights and flowers. Even the ugly teapot received a place in the window.

During Mr. Costa's first song, Pepper escaped again. The cat climbed into the largest paper flower and sat there like a black center. The guests took photos and ordered more cake.

Leo put a small sign under the flower: TODAY'S SPECIAL — COFFEE, CAKE, AND ONE ACCIDENTAL CAT. Nina looked at Mr. Costa. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we should use different bags.” He nodded. Pepper did not agree.
