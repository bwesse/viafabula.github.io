Nina was taking the nine o'clock train to Lindenstadt. Her friend Leo was opening a small café there, and Nina had promised to bring the decorations. She carried a large blue travel bag full of paper flowers, colored lights, and one very ugly golden teapot. The situation was beginning to change.

At the station café, Nina put the bag beside her chair. A man with a red hat sat at the next table. He also had a large blue bag. His bag had a little window on one side, but a scarf was hanging over it.

Nina drank her coffee too quickly. Then the station clock showed 8:56. “Oh no!” she said. She took a blue bag, ran down the stairs, and jumped onto the train just before the doors closed.

The man in the red hat looked up from his newspaper. A moment later, he looked down. The blue bag beside him had a golden teapot sticking out of it.

On the train, Nina found her seat and sent Leo a message: “I am coming. The flowers are safe.” Her phone showed three percent battery. Leo answered, “Perfect. Please do not forget the teapot. My aunt loves it.”

Nina smiled and put the phone away. Then something inside her bag moved.

“The lights must be loose,” she thought. The bag moved again. This time, it also said, “Mrrrow.”
