The storm reached the ridge in minutes. Rain hit Riko's face, and the wind pushed him sideways. He ran faster, but the path became a brown river under his feet. That was when things started to get interesting.

Then his paper bag tore. Moonflower seeds fell into the wet grass. Riko tried to collect them, but the wind carried some over the edge. Soon he could no longer see the path behind him or the meadow ahead.

For the first time that day, Riko stopped talking. He sat under a stone and held the broken bag. “Being fast is not useful when I do not know where to go,” he thought.

Below the ridge, Sela heard someone call her name. She followed the sound to a narrow path between two rocks. Riko was there, wet and cold.

“Did you come to say you were right?” he asked. Sela shook her head. “No. I came because the white stones lead past this place.” She opened one of her protective leaves. Inside, her seed bag was dry.

Sela had also brought an empty leaf pouch. Together, they put Riko's remaining seeds inside it. “I can carry both bags,” said Sela, “but we may arrive late.”

Riko looked toward the white stones. “You show me the way,” he said. “When the path is safe, I will carry the bags.” This time, he waited for Sela's answer.
