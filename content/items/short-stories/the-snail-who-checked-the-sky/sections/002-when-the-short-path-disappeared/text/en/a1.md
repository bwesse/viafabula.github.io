The storm reached the ridge in minutes.
He ran faster, but the path became a brown river under his feet.

Then his paper bag tore.
Soon he could no longer see the path behind him or the meadow ahead.

For the first time that day, Riko stopped talking.
“Being fast is not useful when I do not know where to go,” he thought.

Below the ridge, Sela heard someone call her name.
Riko was there, wet and cold.

“Did you come to say you were right?” he asked.
Inside, her seed bag was dry.

Sela had also brought an empty leaf pouch.
“I can carry both bags,” said Sela, “but we may arrive late.”

Riko looked toward the white stones.
This time, he waited for Sela's answer.
