Before leaving, Sela wrapped her seed bag in two broad leaves. She tied them with a long piece of grass. She also asked Owl to draw the three paths in the earth.

“You will lose time with all that preparation,” said Rabbit. Sela touched the first white stone beside the stream. “Perhaps,” she answered. “But I will know where I am.”

Riko was already far ahead. He raced up the ridge and enjoyed the warm wind behind him. When he passed a group of mice, he ran around them twice, just to show how fast he was.

At the berry bushes, Riko stopped for a snack. “There is plenty of time,” he said. He put the paper seed bag under a bush and told two birds about his great speed.

Sela continued beside the stream. At every crossing, she found a white stone and checked the next direction. The path was longer, but the trees protected her from the wind.

Near noon, the air became cold. The birds left the berry bushes. Riko looked up and saw the first gray cloud above the ridge. “A little rain cannot catch me,” he said.

Thunder answered from the hill. Sela pulled the leaves more tightly around her bag and continued without stopping.
