Sela led them beside the stream. She found each white stone, even when rain covered half of it. Riko walked slowly behind her and did not complain. From that moment, the story moved in a new direction.

When the trees ended, the storm moved east. The final part of the path was clear and dry. Riko placed both leaf pouches on his back. “Now?” he asked. Sela nodded.

Riko ran up the last hill, but he stopped at every turn and looked back. Sela pointed with her feelers. In this way, quick feet and a careful map reached the meadow together before dark.

Owl was waiting under an old tree. She counted the seeds and smiled. Some were missing, but there were enough for the festival. “Which of you won?” asked Rabbit, who had followed Owl to the meadow.

Riko looked at Sela. “The flowers,” he said. Sela added, “And perhaps the weather.” No one understood the second answer, but Owl laughed.

The animals planted the seeds the next morning. Riko carried water quickly from the stream. Before every journey, he asked Sela which path was best. He still liked speed, but now he also liked arriving.

Later that summer, moonflowers appeared on the meadow. A few also grew along the windy ridge where Riko had dropped his seeds. They were smaller, but they shone just as brightly.
