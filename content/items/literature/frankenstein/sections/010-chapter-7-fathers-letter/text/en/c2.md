Chapter 7: The Father’s Letter

At this highly advanced level, the section is framed through its precise narrative and thematic consequences.

Alphonse writes that Victor’s young brother William has been murdered. Victor immediately travels home.

Outside Geneva during a storm, Victor sees the creature and realizes that it killed William. He cannot prove this impossible story.

The authorities accuse Justine because William’s miniature is found in her pocket. Victor remains silent and feels responsible for both victims.

The episode consequently functions as both a narrative turning point and a concentrated statement of the work’s larger concerns.
