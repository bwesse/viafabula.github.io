Chapter 4: The Secret of Life

At this highly advanced level, the section is framed through its precise narrative and thematic consequences.

Victor becomes an exceptional student and isolates himself from family and friends. He studies decay in graves and dissecting rooms.

At last he believes he has discovered how life begins. He decides to build a human body and make it live.

Because small parts are difficult to use, he makes the body enormous. His health weakens while ambition and secrecy control him.

The episode consequently functions as both a narrative turning point and a concentrated statement of the work’s larger concerns.
