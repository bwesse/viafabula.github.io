Chapter 16: Revenge on the Creator

In contemporary prose, the section develops as follows.

After the De Laceys leave, the creature burns their cottage in despair. It travels toward Geneva to find Victor.

It rescues a girl from drowning, but her companion shoots it. The wound turns its desire for love into hatred of humanity.

Near Geneva it kills William after learning that he is Victor’s brother. It hides the miniature on Justine and then demands that Victor create a female companion.

What happens here changes the direction of everything that follows.
