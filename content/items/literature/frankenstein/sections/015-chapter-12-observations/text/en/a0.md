Chapter 12: Observing the Cottagers

The creature watches the De Lacey family: the blind father, Felix, and Agatha. Their poverty and affection move it deeply.

It secretly stops stealing their food and gathers firewood for them at night. By listening, it begins to understand words and emotions.

The family’s kindness teaches it to hope for acceptance. At the same time, their beauty makes it more aware of its own appearance.
