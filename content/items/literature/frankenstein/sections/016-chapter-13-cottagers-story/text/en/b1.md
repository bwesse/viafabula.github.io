Chapter 13: Safie Arrives

This adaptation keeps the central events, motives, and consequences.

A young woman named Safie arrives, and the De Laceys welcome her joyfully. Felix teaches her French.

Listening to the lessons, the creature learns language, history, government, wealth, and social inequality. Knowledge expands both its mind and its pain.

It understands that people have families and positions in society, while it has no name, property, or companion.

The result shapes the choices that follow.
