Chapter 14: The De Laceys’ History

At this highly advanced level, the section is framed through its precise narrative and thematic consequences.

The creature learns why the De Laceys live in exile. Felix helped Safie’s Turkish father escape from prison because he loved Safie and opposed injustice.

Safie’s father later betrayed Felix and tried to take his daughter away. The French government punished the whole De Lacey family.

Safie rejected her father’s plan and travelled alone to Felix. Her courage reunites the lovers, but the family remains poor.

The episode consequently functions as both a narrative turning point and a concentrated statement of the work’s larger concerns.
