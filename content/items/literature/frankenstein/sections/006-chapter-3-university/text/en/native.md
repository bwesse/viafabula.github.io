Chapter 3: University

In contemporary prose, the section develops as follows.

Before Victor leaves for university, Elizabeth catches scarlet fever. Caroline nurses her, becomes ill herself, and dies after asking Victor and Elizabeth to marry one day.

Grieving, Victor travels to Ingolstadt. Professor Krempe mocks his old reading, but Professor Waldman presents modern science as a path to great discoveries.

Waldman’s encouragement gives Victor a new purpose. He begins studying chemistry and anatomy with intense energy.

What happens here changes the direction of everything that follows.
