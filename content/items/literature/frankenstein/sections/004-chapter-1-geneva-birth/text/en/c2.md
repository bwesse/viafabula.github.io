Chapter 1: Family in Geneva

At this highly advanced level, the section is framed through its precise narrative and thematic consequences.

Victor grows up in a respected Geneva family. His father Alphonse marries Caroline after helping her through poverty and grief.

During a journey, the family takes in Elizabeth Lavenza. Victor loves her as his closest companion and imagines that they belong together.

His childhood is protected and affectionate. This happiness makes the later destruction of his family even more painful.

The episode consequently functions as both a narrative turning point and a concentrated statement of the work’s larger concerns.
