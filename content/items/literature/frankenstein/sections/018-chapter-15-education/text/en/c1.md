Chapter 15: Books and Rejection

This advanced adaptation retains the narrative logic, emotional tension, and thematic implications.

The creature finds books and reads about human virtue, suffering, and creation. Victor’s journal reveals how disgusted its creator was at its birth.

It plans to speak first with the blind De Lacey, who cannot judge its face. The old man listens kindly.

Felix, Agatha, and Safie return unexpectedly. Felix attacks the creature, and the terrified family abandons the cottage.

Its consequences deepen the conflict between individual desire, responsibility, and social judgment.
