Chapter 19: Britain and the Islands

This fuller adaptation preserves the section’s central action and ideas.

Victor and Henry travel through England and Scotland. Henry enjoys the people and landscapes, while Victor remains trapped in fear.

Victor leaves his friend and rents a remote hut on an Orkney island. There he begins building the female creature.

The lonely sea and poor surroundings mirror his state of mind. He works slowly, always expecting his enemy to appear.

The section therefore links personal choice with its wider consequences.
