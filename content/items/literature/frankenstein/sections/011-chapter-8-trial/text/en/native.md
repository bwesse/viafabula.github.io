Chapter 8: Justine’s Trial

In contemporary prose, the section develops as follows.

Justine is tried for William’s murder. Elizabeth speaks passionately about her good character, but the evidence convinces the court.

Under religious pressure, Justine gives a false confession and is sentenced to death. She later tells Elizabeth and Victor the truth.

Justine dies innocent. Victor knows his creation caused the crime, and his silence fills him with guilt.

What happens here changes the direction of everything that follows.
