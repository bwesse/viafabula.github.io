Chapter 21: The Magistrate

This version tells the main events in a connected way.

The murdered man is Henry Clerval. When Victor sees his friend’s body, he collapses into fever and delirium.

The Irish magistrate, Mr Kirwin, treats him fairly and contacts Alphonse. Evidence later proves Victor was at sea when Henry died.

Victor is released, but grief and guilt have broken him. He returns toward Geneva with his father and depends on medicine to sleep.

These events prepare the next part of the book.
