Chapter 9: Despair

This adaptation keeps the central events, motives, and consequences.

After the executions, the Frankenstein family sinks into grief. Victor considers death but stays alive for the people who love him.

He feels hunted by the creature and powerless to explain the danger. Elizabeth loses her old faith in human justice.

Victor seeks relief in the Alps. The vast mountains and glaciers briefly quiet his guilt and fear.

The result shapes the choices that follow.
