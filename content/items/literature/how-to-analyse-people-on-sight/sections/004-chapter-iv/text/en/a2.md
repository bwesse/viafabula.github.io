Chapter IV: The Osseous Type

This version tells the main events in a connected way.

The “Osseous” type is identified by a thin frame and prominent bones. The authors connect it with patience, caution, endurance, and respect for rules.

They describe this person as slow to decide but difficult to move after a decision. Advice focuses on facts, time, and dependable routines.

The proposed link between bone structure and temperament has no modern scientific basis. Individual behavior is far more complex.

These events prepare the next part of the book.
