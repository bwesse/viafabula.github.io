Chapter II: The Thoracic Type

At this highly advanced level, the section is framed through its precise narrative and thematic consequences.

The “Thoracic” type is linked to a prominent chest, quick movement, strong emotion, and love of excitement.

The authors portray this person as expressive, charming, changeable, and sensitive to approval. They also assign preferred clothes, jobs, and partners.

The chapter reflects early twentieth-century typology rather than evidence-based psychology. Appearance alone cannot establish these qualities.

The episode consequently functions as both a narrative turning point and a concentrated statement of the work’s larger concerns.
