Chapter V: The Cerebral Type

In contemporary prose, the section develops as follows.

The “Cerebral” type is associated with a large head, slight body, intense thought, imagination, and sensitivity.

The authors say this person prefers ideas to physical action, needs privacy, and may neglect comfort while concentrating. They recommend intellectual work and quiet communication.

This description mixes observation with unsupported inference. Head or body shape does not reveal intelligence, talent, or character.

What happens here changes the direction of everything that follows.
