Chapter I: The Alimentive Type

In contemporary prose, the section develops as follows.

The authors describe an “Alimentive” type associated with a round, soft body and enjoyment of comfort, food, and company.

They claim that this person is friendly, adaptable, and interested in immediate pleasure, but may avoid effort or conflict.

The chapter then recommends ways to work, sell, and communicate with this supposed type. Its body-to-character conclusions are stereotypes, not reliable diagnosis.

What happens here changes the direction of everything that follows.
